1215

Magna Carta

In 1215, King John of England was forced by his subjects to sign the Magna Carta after violating ancient laws and traditions.

This document is considered one of the first major steps toward limiting royal power and establishing the foundation of modern human rights and democracy.

Main Provisions:

    1. Freedom of the Church from governmental interference.

    2. The right of citizens to own and inherit property.

    3. Protection from excessive taxation.

    4. Rights of widows to choose not to remarry.

    5. The principles of due process and equality before the law.

    6. The prohibition of bribery and official misconduct.
